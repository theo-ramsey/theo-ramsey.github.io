Case Study: Retail Data Automation & Decisioning

End-to-end POS-to-dashboard stack built for a retail operator. This case study shows the data foundation, reporting model, real operating dashboards, and a clearly-labeled synthetic promo lift template.

Outcome Replaced a $30K/year bookkeeping role through automation after the role holder departed.
Capability Integrated multiple datasets into a reporting layer to enable drilldowns from store → department → SKU.
Marketing Analysis Relevance Demonstrates the mechanics of proof-of-performance reporting (lift, margin impact, ROI story).

1The Business Problem

Raw POS exports were not decision-ready, trend visibility was limited, and bookkeeping depended on a single role (key-person risk). The goal was to build a repeatable system that produced trusted KPIs and operator-ready views.

2Data Foundation: Integrated Sources

Built a reporting layer on top of normalized sources (daily item sales facts + product master + department/vendor reference data).

3Data Model: Facts + Dimensions (Blended Dataset)

Blended Daily PLU Sales (facts) with PLU master (cost/retail/vendor) and department reference data to enable consistent KPI rollups and drilldowns.

4Business Outputs: Dashboards That Drive Decisions (Real Data)

Operator-ready dashboards used for inventory focus, SKU velocity diagnostics, and identifying profit drivers versus dead inventory.

5Marketing Data Analysis-Relevant Extension: Promo Lift Measurement Template (Synthetic)

The historical dataset did not include promotions. To demonstrate marketing measurement competency and proof-of-performance storytelling, a promo lift dashboard template was created using synthetic promo events and synthetic results.

Template dataset (synthetic): Promo events and results shown are synthetic and provided only to demonstrate measurement design, KPI structure, and reporting storytelling. They are not derived from the real POS dataset.

6Business Impact

  • Automated bookkeeping workflows after a staff departure, avoiding a $30K/year backfill.
  • Created repeatable KPI reporting and trend visibility to support inventory and operational decisioning.
  • Enabled drilldowns from store → department → SKU to identify drivers and reduce dead inventory.
  • Demonstrated a proof-of-performance reporting structure aligned to retail marketing networks (via synthetic template).
